Information for Journalists

Interviewer: "What attracted you to drone photography and how did you get started with it?"
Me (thinking): "Damn, that's a loaded question."

Recently I've been very fortunate to be featured by NBC 10, Curbed Philly, Philadelphia Inquirer, Philly Voice, Penn Gazette, Penn Office of University Communications, and the Tab Penn. In interviews, I am often asked what was my inspiration and path to becoming a commercial drone pilot. I explain my story about my passion since high school for camera tech, and when my friend sold me his broken DJI Phantom 2 drone in December 2015 for a great steal, I fixed it at 4am the next morning. I talk about Drone Eddie from Huntington Beach, CA, and him inspiring me to make drone photos available to the community.

What's often left out of the story, however, are the numerous challenges I've faced working on over 50 drone and video projects before becoming FAA certified. I figured it would be most effective for me to convey my story visually. I created this page for journalists. It is not publicly searchable, and there is no link to here from the home page of the website. Feel free to email me at phillybydrone@gmail.com or call me at (714) 855-5951 with any questions.

-Christopher Kao
